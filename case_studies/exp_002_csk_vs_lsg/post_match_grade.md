The memo explicitly noted the market sat "at the low edge of the band" — a valuable observation confirmed by the result. The Skeptic's base-rate buildup to 57–61% was more accurate than the Polymarket price.

LSG's 3W-6L reflected genuine team weakness — they were eliminated from playoff contention after this loss.

- **Toss alignment and bowl-first reasoning were sound.** The memo correctly identified that CSK winning the toss and bowling first on a rain-affected pitch aligned with recent Chepauk trends (58.6% chase win rate 2023-2026). CSK chasing was the right side of the innings split, and the actual chase succeeded.

The memo appropriately hedged around post-rain pitch behavior and impact player selections. No unsourced claims were laundered into the band.

### What the memo got wrong

- **The Inglis injury concern was directionally wrong.** The memo listed "Inglis fails early due to thumb injury and LSG's top order cascades" as a scenario that would confirm the directional thesis (pushing CSK toward 60-63%). In reality, Inglis scored 85 off 33 balls (10 fours, 6 sixes) — one of the most explosive innings in IPL 2026, with a half-century off just 17 balls. His thumb injury had zero observable impact on his batting. The memo's framing of injury-returning players as directional vulnerabilities was unsupported once the player was confirmed in the XI by the captain at toss.

- **The "190+ shifts to coin-flip" conditional was poorly calibrated.** The "What Would Change This View" section stated: "LSG post 190+: Would shift band downward toward CSK 50-55%." LSG posted 203/8 — well above the 190 threshold — yet CSK chased it down with 5 wickets and 4 balls to spare, powered by Urvil Patel's record-equalling 65 off 23 balls (8 sixes). The conditional assumed a high LSG total would neutralize CSK's structural advantages, but failed to account for two factors: (a) the same flat Chepauk conditions that enabled LSG's 203 also enabled CSK's high-scoring chase, and (b) CSK's batting firepower (Samson 402 runs at 163.4 SR, Urvil Patel's explosiveness) was capable of chasing 200+. The average first-innings score at Chepauk in 2026 was 186 — posting 203 was above par but not the insurmountable barrier the conditional implied.

- **The spin advantage was overemphasized relative to the actual winning mechanism.** The memo's Key Reasoning point #2 devoted the most analytical depth to "CSK's compound spin and bowling advantage on a spin-friendly surface," citing spin economy differentials and the multiplicative effect of LSG's bowling downgrade. In the actual match, the decisive bowling performance was Jamie Overton's 3/36 (pace) — he triggered LSG's collapse from 91/1 to 130/5 by removing Pant and Inglis in the same over. Noor Ahmad was indeed economical (4-0-24-1), but spin was a containment factor, not the match-winning mechanism. The memo correctly identified the structural advantage but overweighted it relative to the individual pace bowling quality that actually decided the match.

### What was missed

- **Urvil Patel's match-winning potential was invisible in the analysis.** Patel did not appear in the top-5 batters list in the stats snapshot (Samson, Gaikwad, Mhatre, SN Khan, Kartik Sharma). His 65 off 23 balls — equalling Jaiswal's record for the fastest IPL fifty at 13 balls — was the innings that made a 204-run chase look routine. The stats snapshot's top-5 filter systematically excludes lower-order explosive batters who may not accumulate season runs but can be match-decisive. This is an extension of the Reflection Log Entry 4 pattern (all-rounders' batting contributions being missed), but applied to lower-order impact players.

- **CSK's ability to chase 200+ was underestimated.** The memo noted this was CSK's first successful 200+ chase since 2018 — but didn't realize it was coming. The conditional framing ("190+ shifts band toward coin-flip") treated high targets as a leveling mechanism. But CSK's squad contained Samson (163.4 SR), Mhatre (168.9 SR), and Patel — a batting lineup with the raw strike rates to chase virtually any total on a flat pitch. The memo's bowling-centric thesis (spin advantage + bowling downgrade compound = lower LSG total) crowded out the equally important question: "If LSG post 200, can CSK chase it?"

- **The pitch played flat despite the rain/covers narrative.** LSG scored 91/1 in the powerplay — their highest-ever powerplay score in IPL history. The post-rain seam assistance that Gaikwad anticipated and the memo flagged as the "Main Uncertainty" simply did not materialize. The memo correctly labeled this as speculative and unquantifiable, but the overall framing still leaned into the bowl-first narrative. In reality, conditions were excellent for batting throughout both innings.

### Decisive Factor

Overton's 3/36 had earlier triggered LSG's middle-order collapse (91/1 to 130/5), limiting the damage from Inglis's extraordinary 85(33). The memo correctly identified CSK as favorites but through the wrong primary mechanism: it emphasized spin bowling advantage and LSG's bowling downgrade, while the match was won through individual pace bowling brilliance (Overton) and explosive batting (Patel) — neither of which were central to the memo's thesis.

## Lessons for Reflection Log

- **When a player returning from injury is confirmed in the playing XI at toss, do not treat the injury as a directional vulnerability.** Team selection implies medical clearance. Inglis was confirmed by Pant at toss and scored 85(33) — the most explosive innings of the match. The memo flagged "Inglis fails early due to thumb injury" as a scenario favoring CSK, but once selected, the prior shifts from "injured player might underperform" to "team physios cleared him for match conditions." Discount injury concerns by at least 75% once the player is in the confirmed XI; reserve injury-based adjustments for cases where the player is a genuine game-time decision (not yet selected).

- **When a venue has a high average first-innings score in the current season, conditional scenarios tied to scoring thresholds must account for the chasing team benefiting from the same conditions.** Chepauk's 2026 average first innings was 186 runs. The memo treated "LSG posts 190+" as a band-shifting scenario toward coin-flip, but the same conditions that enable a 200+ first innings also enable a 200+ chase. A high total on a flat, high-scoring pitch is a fundamentally different signal than a high total on a deteriorating surface. Calibrate conditionals against the venue's scoring environment, not against a generic difficulty threshold.
