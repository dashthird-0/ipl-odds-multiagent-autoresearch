The historical average first-innings score of 146 (6 matches, decade-old — CricTracker 2026-05-09; CREX 2026-05-10) diverges by nearly 40 runs from unsourced pundit expectations of 180-190. The pitch has been described as both a "flat deck" and "slightly slower end" in contradictory terms, with no curator comments available (Source Quality: speculative). Boundary dimensions of 80m+ (two dated sources — Source Quality: probable) may limit six-hitting and favor ground-stroke batting, but how this interacts with the unknown surface is unpredictable.

The band is set at **52-62% RCB (width: 10pp, approximately plus/minus 5pp from midpoint ~57%)**, which is consistent with the Skeptic's recommended plus/minus 8pp applied to the market anchor of 55.5% (yielding 47.5-63.5%), narrowed slightly because certain factors are well-established. This width reflects:

1. **Venue data void.** The stats_snapshot.json venue splits are inapplicable (wrong ground), and Raipur's decade-old 6-match IPL history provides no usable anchor. Per Reflection Log Entry 1, when venue-specific data is absent or contradictory, structural priors must be heavily discounted. Here they are not merely contradicted — they are nonexistent. This alone justifies widening beyond a normal match band.

2. **Unresolved MI personnel.** Two key players (Pandya, SKY) are unconfirmed. The Source Quality Clerk rates both as probable-to-speculative. The XI that takes the field could range from MI's best available lineup to a significantly weakened configuration with emergency captaincy.

3. **Contradictory pitch intelligence.** "Flat deck" versus "slightly slower end" cannot both be true. No curator comments exist. Historical averages (146) versus pundit expectations (180-190) create a 40-run gap that propagates into match-outcome uncertainty.

4. **Moderate market liquidity.** The $35K Polymarket volume with a 1% bid-ask spread provides a directional anchor but is not deep enough to treat the market price as highly efficient.

The lower bound of 52% reflects a scenario where Raipur conditions neutralize RCB's bowling advantage, MI field their strongest available XI, and the venue's unfamiliarity erases any RCB structural advantage. The upper bound of 62% reflects a scenario where the toss+dew advantage is fully realized, RCB's batting depth (Kohli 379 runs SR 156.6, Patidar 318 runs SR 186.0, Padikkal 316 runs SR 177.5) capitalizes on MI's leaky bowling, and Pandya's absence compounds MI's weakness. The market anchor of 55.5% sits comfortably within this band, slightly below the form-derived midpoint of ~57%.
