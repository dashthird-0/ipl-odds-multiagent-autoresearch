# Post-Match Grade: Gujarat Titans vs Sunrisers Hyderabad
## 2026-05-12, Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad

**Result:** Gujarat Titans won by 82 runs (GT 168/5 in 20 overs; SRH 86 all out in 14.5 overs). SRH's lowest-ever IPL total.
**Memo directional view:** GT slightly favored. Market appeared to be overweighting toss/dew narrative against venue-specific evidence. Midpoint ~55% GT vs market's 45.5% GT.
**Band:** GT 49-61% (midpoint ~55%) — Result **well within band.** GT won by 82 runs — the largest victory margin in GT's IPL history. The dominance of the outcome suggests the true probability was at or above the upper end of the band. The memo's 9.5pp contrarian gap over the market was in the correct direction and, if anything, too conservative.

## Reasoning Grade: A-

### What the memo got right

- **The contrarian call against the market was the memo's defining strength, and it was vindicated emphatically.** The market had GT at 45.5% post-toss. The memo placed GT at 55% — a 9.5pp contrarian gap. GT won by 82 runs, bowling SRH out for their lowest-ever IPL total. This is the first experiment in the series where the memo's directional view was both (a) strongly contrarian to the market and (b) correct by a large margin. The memo explicitly stated: "market appears to be overweighting toss/dew narrative against venue-specific evidence." This was exactly right. The memo correctly applied Entry 8 (lean toward base-rate estimate when analysis supports divergence from market) — the base-rate analysis pointed to GT 52-57%, and the memo adopted 55% rather than drifting back toward the market's 45.5%.

- **The toss/dew challenge was the single most valuable analytical contribution and was completely validated.** The memo's reasoning point #2 stated: "the toss winner who elects to field first wins only 50.0% of the time (15/30, 2023-2026)" at NMS, and flagged humidity at 11-20% with a 23C dew-point gap making significant dew "physically unlikely." The market moved 4pp toward SRH post-toss. The memo assessed this swing as 2-3pp excessive. In the event, SRH won the toss, chose to field, and lost by 82 runs. The dew did not materialize as a factor — GT's bowling dominated in the second innings with no evident wet-ball disadvantage. The Skeptic's Challenge #1 was the most prescient observation in the entire evidence chain.

- **The H2H dominance at this venue was correctly identified as the strongest prior and was vindicated.** The memo stated: "GT have won 5 of 6 completed meetings against SRH, and critically, all 3 at Narendra Modi Stadium. In two of those three NMS matches, SRH won the toss and chose to field first — exactly the same situation as tonight — and still lost." GT extended to 6-1 overall and 4-0 at NMS. The memo explicitly called out the pattern of SRH fielding first at NMS and losing — this exact scenario repeated. The confidence interval around 6 matches is wide, but the directional signal was consistent, venue-specific, and condition-specific (across different toss outcomes and years). The memo correctly gave this more weight than the market did.

- **GT's bowling quality was correctly identified as the key dynamic.** Reasoning point #4 stated: "GT have the powerplay bowling quality (Rabada, Siraj, with 21 powerplay wickets) to attack SRH's aggressive opening. Rashid Khan provides middle-overs control... GT's bowling has historically prevailed in this fixture." In the match, Rabada took 3/28 (including Abhishek, Ishan, and Smaran in the powerplay), Holder took 3/20 (including Klaasen), Siraj bowled 3-1-11-1 (dismissing Travis Head for 0 in the first over), and Prasidh Krishna (impact sub) took 2/23. SRH lost 4 wickets in the powerplay and were bowled out in 14.5 overs. The memo's bowling-quality thesis was the mechanism that produced the result.

- **Travis Head's non-starting status was correctly assessed using Entry 3 (model the actual XI).** The memo stated: "Head (361 runs, SR 165.6) is an impact sub, not starting. Per Reflection Log Entry 3, we must assess the actual XI, not season averages that include Head." This was validated: SRH's starting batting (without Head) was weaker, and when Head was brought in as impact sub for the chase, he scored 0 off 4 balls, dismissed by Siraj in the very first over. The memo's observation that "SRH's starting batting is weaker than aggregate stats suggest" was borne out — the starting lineup managed only 86 all out.

- **The main uncertainty was correctly identified and correctly resolved.** The memo stated: "Whether SRH's 2026 squad composition makes the historical H2H irrelevant." The memo judged that it probably didn't, weighting H2H as the strongest prior. This was correct. SRH's upgraded batting lineup (Abhishek SR 188.5, Ishan SR 181.0, Klaasen SR 149.2) was bowled out for 86 — the same pattern of GT bowling dominance at this venue persisted despite SRH's improved roster.

- **Venue data contamination was correctly handled for the fourth consecutive experiment.** The stats_snapshot showed 0 matches at NMS due to the name-matching issue. The memo identified this gap, sourced venue data through the Skeptic's Cricsheet analysis (31 matches, 48.4% chase win rate), and used the journalistic H2H data to fill the venue-specific gap. The pipeline's venue data hygiene is now a consistent strength.

### What the memo got wrong

- **The midpoint of 55% was too conservative given the strength of the evidence, and the memo should have been closer to 58-60%.** The Skeptic's anchoring check synthesized: H2H at venue (+5-8pp from 50/50 base), home advantage (+2-3pp), negligible toss advantage (+0-1pp SRH), SRH season quality (+1-2pp SRH) = net GT ~52-57%. The memo settled at 55%, the middle of the Skeptic's range. But the evidence supporting the upper end was stronger than the evidence supporting the lower end: (1) the H2H was 5-1 overall and 3-0 at venue, not 4-2 — the consistency warranted +7-8pp, not +5pp; (2) the toss/dew adjustment should have been 0pp, not +0-1pp SRH, given the weather data; (3) GT's bowling quality advantage was structural, not contingent. A midpoint of 58% would have been more defensible and would have better reflected the weight of evidence. The 82-run margin suggests the true pre-match probability may have been even higher.

- **The band placement was slightly low.** A band of 52-64% (same 12pp width, shifted 3pp toward GT) would have better reflected the evidence. The lower bound of 49% gave too much credence to the "SRH's squad upgrade makes H2H irrelevant" hypothesis — but the memo's own analysis had already partially dismissed this by noting GT's bowling attack (Rabada, Siraj, Rashid) was "also elite and has consistently handled high-quality batting lineups at this venue." The lower bound should have been held at 52%, not 49%.

- **The "What Would Change This View" scenarios didn't model the GT bowling dominance pathway.** The scenarios focused on: (1) SRH's powerplay aggression surviving GT's pace; (2) GT posting 200+; (3) Head entering as impact sub; (4) dew materializing. None modeled the scenario that actually occurred: GT posting a below-average total (168, vs NMS average of ~193) but defending it comfortably because of overwhelming bowling quality. The absence of a "GT's bowling restricts SRH to <130 even on a moderate total" scenario reveals that the memo was somewhat anchored on the batting-first total as the key determinant, when in fact GT's bowling was the dominant factor regardless of total.

- **GT's impact sub advantage was under-modeled relative to SRH's.** The memo discussed SRH's impact sub options (Head +1-2pp if used in chase) but didn't equivalently model GT's option. GT used Prasidh Krishna (2/23 in 3 overs), who removed Salil Arora and Pat Cummins — two of the last batsmen offering resistance. The memo had flagged Prasidh as having 12 wickets at 9.96 economy in the stats snapshot. This was available evidence that should have been weighted symmetrically with the Head analysis. Entry 3's application note (from Exp 4) about modeling replacements reciprocally for both teams applies here to impact subs as well.

### What was missed

- **SRH's away record deserved more granular analysis.** SRH's 6-of-7 winning run was impressive, but 4 of those 6 wins were at home in Hyderabad. Their away wins during this stretch were at Jaipur (vs RR) and Mumbai (vs MI) — neither against a top-4 team at a hostile venue. The memo noted SRH's "away: 3-2" record but didn't decompose the quality of opposition or conditions in those away matches. Traveling to Ahmedabad (extreme heat, hostile crowd, GT's bowling at their home ground) was a different test than Jaipur or Mumbai. A home/away decomposition of the recent run would have further supported the GT-favored view.

- **The heatwave conditions (44-45C daytime high) were flagged in the news packet but not translated into an analytical adjustment.** The news report noted a "heatwave alert in Ahmedabad" and mentioned potential "player fatigue despite the evening start." GT, playing at home, would be more acclimatized. SRH, traveling from Hyderabad after 6 days rest, were entering extreme conditions. While this is speculative and small-effect, the fielding-first team (SRH) would have spent the most energy in the hottest part of the evening. This environmental factor was mentioned but not integrated into the probability assessment.

- **The impact sub as a "chase deployment" should have been modeled differently from a structural advantage.** The memo treated Head's potential impact sub entry as a structural advantage (+1-2pp SRH). But deploying an impact sub to open the batting in a chase of 169 — facing GT's new-ball attack cold — is a different situation from deploying Head into a chase of 220+ where his strike rate is the differentiator. Head faced Siraj with no time to settle and was dismissed for 0 off 4 balls. The context of deployment (total to chase, match pressure, bowling attack to face) matters more than the fact of availability. A more nuanced model would have noted that Head's impact sub value is highest in high-scoring chases and lower in modest ones where the new-ball attack is the primary threat.

### Skeptic Assessment

The Skeptic's work was the strongest component of the evidence chain for the third consecutive experiment — and in this match, the Skeptic provided the analytical framework that produced the memo's best call.

**Strengths:** (1) Challenge #1 (toss overpricing) was the most valuable single contribution in the entire experiment series to date. The 50.0% field-first win rate at NMS, combined with the weather-data debunk of the dew narrative, was the foundation of the memo's contrarian call. The Skeptic identified that the "4pp market swing overstates the toss value by approximately 2-3pp at this venue" — the actual outcome (82-run loss for the fielding side) suggests the overstatement was even larger. (2) Challenge #4 (H2H supported with wide uncertainty) correctly identified the 5-1 H2H as a genuine signal while noting sample-size limitations. The Skeptic recommended +3-5pp from the base rate for H2H, which was appropriate and validated. (3) The anchoring check was rigorous: starting from 50/50, building up adjustments for H2H (+5-8pp GT), home advantage (+2-3pp GT), SRH quality (+1-2pp SRH), toss (+0-1pp SRH) to arrive at GT ~52-57%. This systematic approach produced a more accurate estimate than the market. (4) Challenge #6 (Head's absence overstated) was prescient — Head entered the match and scored 0. (5) The Entry 8 application note correctly instructed the Synthesizer to lean toward the base-rate estimate. (6) Challenges #2 and #3 (momentum/streak claims unsupported) correctly neutralized both teams' form narratives using the 50.0% base rate for 4+ win streaks.

**Weakness:** The Skeptic correctly identified the gap between base rates (52-57% GT) and market (45.5% GT) but then offered "What might the market be pricing that our stats miss?" — noting SRH's explosive batting, squad quality upgrade, and impact sub depth. These were valid considerations, but the Skeptic's own data had already addressed each: (a) the explosive batting lineup had faced GT at NMS before and lost; (b) squad quality upgrades don't explain a 9-12pp deviation when the base-rate adjustment for quality was only +1-2pp SRH; (c) the impact sub upside was already modeled at +1-2pp. The Skeptic weakened its own contrarian case by lending too much credibility to the market's pricing logic. When the Skeptic's systematic analysis produces a 7-12pp gap and the gap is explained by identifiable venue-specific factors, the section explaining "what the market might know" should be shorter and more skeptical of the market, not deferential to it.

### Decisive Factor

The match was decided in two phases. First, GT's middle-order recovery: from 26/2 in the powerplay (Gill 5, Buttler 7), Sudharsan (61 off 44) and Washington Sundar (50 off 33) added 60 runs for the 4th wicket, pushing GT to a defensible 168/5. Second, and decisively, GT's pace attack destroyed SRH in the powerplay: Siraj dismissed Head (impact sub, 0 off 4) in the first over, Rabada removed Abhishek (6), Ishan (11), and Smaran (9) in a 3-wicket powerplay burst, and Holder cleaned up the middle order including Klaasen (14). SRH were 32/4 after the powerplay and never recovered, bowled out for 86 in 14.5 overs.

The memo's core thesis — that GT's bowling quality would prevail against SRH's batting at this venue, as it had in three previous encounters — was exactly what happened. The specific pathway (below-par total defended by overwhelming bowling) was not the memo's base scenario (which implicitly assumed GT would need a 200+ score), but the underlying causal mechanism (GT bowling > SRH batting at NMS) was correctly identified. The memo would have scored higher if it had explicitly modeled the "GT bowling dominates regardless of first-innings total" scenario, but the analytical foundation was sound.

## Lessons for Reflection Log

- **When same-day weather data contradicts a venue's generic "dew advantage" narrative AND venue-level field-first win rate is ≤50%, cap the toss adjustment at 0pp and treat any market swing >2pp as overpricing the toss.** At NMS the field-first win rate was 50% (15/30). Humidity was 11-20% with a 23C dew-point gap. The market moved 4pp toward SRH post-toss. SRH chose to field and lost by 82 runs. Venue-specific toss data combined with same-day weather data is more informative than generic evening dew narratives. This is the strongest case so far of the market systematically overpricing a toss.

- **When a team's H2H dominance at a specific venue is ≥3-0 across different toss outcomes and years, weight it at the upper end of the +5-8pp adjustment range — opponent squad improvements reduce but do not eliminate the prior.** GT's 3-0 at NMS (including 2 matches where SRH chose to field first) extended to 4-0. SRH's 2026 batting lineup (strongest they've ever fielded) was bowled out for 86. Venue-specific H2H captures persistent dynamics — bowling attack vs batting style, pitch familiarity, crowd effect — that survive opponent squad upgrades more than market participants expect.
