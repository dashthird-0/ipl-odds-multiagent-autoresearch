## Main Uncertainty

**The toss has not happened, and this is a potentially toss-sensitive venue.** Although the Raipur chase win rate of 71.4% (5/7) is from a tiny sample that cannot be statistically distinguished from 50% (Skeptic, Challenge 6; 95% CI ~29-96%), the combination of dew rising through the evening (humidity 29% to 61%), larger boundaries (~80m), and a slow surface that rewards second-innings knowledge of conditions creates a plausible directional toss advantage for the bowling-first side. Per Entry 9 of the reflection log, same-day weather data (moderate humidity, dew-point gap of ~15-20C at match start) suggests moderate, not extreme, dew -- so we cap the toss adjustment at +3-5pp for whichever team chases. Pre-toss, this widens the band symmetrically rather than favoring either side. If RCB win the toss and bowl first, the upper end of the band (62%) activates. If KKR win and bowl first, the lower end (46%) becomes relevant.

## What Would Change This View

- **Toss result and chase assignment.** If RCB win toss and bowl first: band shifts to RCB 56-62%. If KKR win toss and bowl first: band shifts to RCB 46-52%. This is the single largest swing factor (~6pp directional).
- **Confirmed playing XIs.** If Phil Salt is confirmed fit and plays, add +2-3pp to RCB. If Varun Chakravarthy is ruled out, add +1-2pp to RCB. If Finn Allen starts (not impact sub), add +1-2pp to KKR -- his 100 off 47 vs DC (CricketAddictor) makes his powerplay presence material.
- **Match shortened to under 15 overs per side.** Reduced-overs formats increase variance, compress the spin advantage (fewer middle-overs), and slightly favor the team with more explosive top-order power. This would modestly favor KKR if Allen opens.
- **Washout.** If the match is abandoned (5-8% probability), both teams get 1 point -- relatively more valuable to KKR's playoff arithmetic than RCB's. A washout effectively removes the match from probability-band analysis.

- **No toss result** at a venue with a plausible (though statistically uncertain) chase advantage. The toss alone could swing the estimate by ~6pp in either direction.

- **Conflicting weather signals.** Washout risk is 5-8%, not negligible. Dew intensity is uncertain.
- **The midpoint sits at 54%, not the market's 57.5%.** Per Entry 8 of the reflection log, our analysis consistently produces RCB 53-55% through multiple independent paths (standings quality adjustment, streak offset, Bhuvneshwar edge, Salt/Varun netting). The market's 3.5pp premium is explainable by anchoring to RCB's table position and name recognition (Kohli, Patidar) rather than by factors our base-rate construction missed. We do not split the difference; we lean toward the base rate. The band is centered at 54% and the market's 57.5% falls within it but above our midpoint.
