# Post-Match Grade: Punjab Kings vs Mumbai Indians
## 2026-05-14, HPCA Stadium, Dharamsala

**Result:** Mumbai Indians won by 6 wickets (MI 205/4 in 19.5 overs chasing PBKS 200/8 in 20 overs). Tilak Varma 75*(33), Prabhsimran Singh 57(32), Rickelton 48(23), Omarzai 38(17). Shardul Thakur 4/39, Deepak Chahar 2/36, Omarzai 2/36. MI won toss and bowled first. Bumrah captained; Pandya and Suryakumar Yadav both absent. Rohit Sharma used as impact sub (not starting XI).
**Memo directional view:** PBKS moderately favored. Market overpriced PBKS. Midpoint ~57% PBKS vs market's 61.5% PBKS.
**Band:** PBKS 53-61% (MI 39-47%) — Result **outside band.** MI won as a 39-47% underdog. The memo's conditional scenario ("If MI win toss and field first: Narrow the gap to approximately 50-55% [PBKS]") activated. The post-realization conditional estimate places MI at 45-50% — the upper edge of the band barely touches this. The memo's contrarian lean from the market was in the correct direction (MI more likely than 38.5%) but insufficient in magnitude. This is the first band miss in 7 experiments.

## Reasoning Grade: C+

### What the memo got right

- **The contrarian lean from market was directionally correct and well-justified.** The market had PBKS at 61.5%. The memo placed PBKS at 57% (midpoint), attributing the gap to the H2H parity (17-17), inflated home advantage, and the rejected dead-rubber narrative. MI won. The memo's midpoint (MI 43%) was closer to the outcome than the market's (MI 38.5%). Entry 8 was applied correctly in direction: the gap was explained by concrete analytical findings and the memo leaned toward the base-rate estimate. However, the lean was insufficiently aggressive — the correct answer was even further from the market.

- **The dead-rubber dismissal was the single best analytical call in this experiment.** The memo explicitly stated: "no probability adjustment for MI's 'lack of motivation'" (point 4), citing Entry 7 and the Source Quality Clerk's speculative rating. MI — virtually eliminated, missing their captain Pandya and vice-captain Suryakumar Yadav, with Rohit as impact sub rather than starter — won by 6 wickets. Bumrah captained a side that market participants would have heavily discounted. This is the third consecutive experiment (Exp 4, Exp 5, now Exp 7) validating the principle that dead-rubber teams are unpredictable, not systematically weaker. The memo resisted a narrative that would have pushed PBKS even higher and compounded the error.

- **MI's XI uncertainty was correctly identified as the largest unresolved variable and handled per Entry 12.** The memo refused to build detailed matchups around speculative XIs: "Per learning log Entry 12, detailed matchup analysis against unconfirmed XIs creates fragile reasoning. I assess team-level quality only." This was vindicated — the actual MI XI differed significantly from the last known XI (Pandya out, SKY out, Rohit demoted to impact sub, Thakur returned). The memo's team-level assessment ("MI's bowling when Bumrah + Ghazanfar play is competitive") was partially validated (Bumrah played, Ghazanfar didn't, but Thakur compensated with 4/39). The decision to avoid fragile specificity was methodologically sound.

- **The H2H parity anchor (17-17) was the correct analytical foundation.** The memo's strongest reasoning point was using the all-time 17-17 H2H as the base, then building adjustments from there. This prevented the team-quality and table-position signals from pushing PBKS above 60%. The result — a close match won by the "weaker" team by 1 ball — is consistent with a near-50% contest, which the H2H base predicted better than the market's 61.5%.

- **The "What Would Change" conditional framework was directionally accurate.** The memo listed four scenarios. The one that activated was "If MI win toss and field first: Narrow the gap to approximately 50-55% [PBKS]." MI won the toss and bowled. The match outcome (MI won narrowly) is consistent with a 50-55% PBKS estimate for this scenario — the conditional estimate was more accurate than the unconditional midpoint. This is the second consecutive experiment (after Exp 6) where the conditional framework provided better resolution than the point estimate.

- **PBKS's losing streak adjustment was in the correct direction, though insufficiently weighted.** The memo applied -1-2pp for PBKS's 3-match losing streak, noting the losses were "to top-4 teams, mostly away." The result extended the streak to 4 consecutive losses. While the memo correctly identified that the quality of opponents mitigated the streak's significance, it underestimated the possibility of genuine decline rather than opponent-quality effects. The -1-2pp adjustment captured the right direction but needed to be -3-4pp.

### What the memo got wrong

- **The venue identification was wrong, and the pipeline failed to catch it.** The memo analyzed the match as occurring at Maharaja Yadavindra Singh International Cricket Stadium (Mullanpur/New Chandigarh). The match was actually played at HPCA Stadium, Dharamsala. Every venue-specific data point in the memo — the 2026 scoring average of 214.2, the chase split (8-7 in 15 matches), the toss-winner rate of 66.7% (n=15), PBKS's 3-1 home record in 2026, the "high-scoring venue" characterization — was for the wrong ground. This is particularly damaging because: (a) In Exp 6, correct venue identification was praised as "the most important analytical judgment" and a "proven strength" of the pipeline. The pipeline regressed on the exact capability that was its strongest. (b) PBKS's record at Dharamsala was 6-8 entering this match (from Exp 4's analysis) — identical to the 6-8 at Mullanpur, so the Skeptic's home-advantage challenge (#2) happened to reach the same conclusion for the wrong reason. (c) Dharamsala's pitch characteristics (pace-friendly altitude venue, historically different scoring norms from Mullanpur's flat batting surface) differ from Mullanpur's. The "2026 scoring surge to 214" that anchored the venue analysis was Mullanpur-specific data applied to a Dharamsala match. (d) No agent — not the news analyst, not the stats analyst, not the Source Quality Clerk — flagged the venue discrepancy. The Clerk rated Cricsheet data as "confirmed, high-quality" without verifying whether the venue assignment was correct for today's fixture.

- **The home advantage was overestimated despite the correct methodology.** The memo gave PBKS +3-4pp for "moderate home advantage in 2026 at this venue" (meaning Mullanpur's 3-1 record). At the actual venue (Dharamsala), PBKS's record was 6-8 before this match — a home DISADVANTAGE. Entry 1 from the learning log was correctly invoked for Mullanpur (6-8 overall contradicted by 3-1 in 2026). But at Dharamsala, PBKS's persistent underperformance (now 6-9) suggests a genuine structural disadvantage, not a contradiction with recent form. The net home advantage should have been 0pp or slightly negative, not +3-4pp. This alone shifts the band 3-5pp toward MI.

- **The band width was appropriate but the center was too far from 50%.** Given the memo's own acknowledgment of "mixed evidence quality," "complete absence of cricket press sources," MI's "highly unstable" XI, and a "thin liquidity" market anchor — all of which justified a wide band — the center should have been closer to the H2H-implied 50%. The band of 53-61% (center 57%) was essentially saying "PBKS is favored regardless of how MI's uncertainties resolve." A more appropriate band given the acknowledged uncertainties would be 49-59% PBKS (center 54%), which would have captured the MI win within the band.

- **Shardul Thakur was cited as evidence of MI's bowling degradation but took 4/39.** The memo noted that "when [MI] rotate in weaker options, it degrades significantly" and cited "Thakur 11.63 eco" as evidence of poor alternatives. Thakur returned to this XI and was the match's best bowler — 4/39, triggering PBKS's middle-order collapse (his scalps: Prabhsimran Singh, Iyer, Shedge, Jansen). Season economy rates describe central tendency, not ceiling. A bowler with 11.63 economy can still produce a 4-wicket haul on his day. The memo's treatment of Thakur as a categorical downgrade rather than a high-variance option understated MI's ceiling scenario.

- **Bumrah's 0/35 (economy 8.75) went against the memo's strongest MI bull case.** The memo's key reasoning about MI stated: "MI's bowling when Bumrah + Ghazanfar play is competitive." Bumrah played but returned his most expensive IPL figures of the season. Ghazanfar didn't play. Yet MI still won — because Thakur and Chahar (2/36) compensated. The memo correctly identified Bumrah as critical but didn't model a scenario where Bumrah was neutralized but other bowlers stepped up. The team-level quality assessment was too concentrated on one player.

### What was missed

- **The venue change from Mullanpur to Dharamsala was not detected by any agent in the pipeline.** The schedule apparently had this match at Dharamsala (multiple sources confirm), yet the stats analyst queried Mullanpur data, the news analyst described Mullanpur weather and pitch conditions, and no agent cross-checked the IPL schedule against the assumed venue. In Exp 6, the venue discrepancy was caught because the news agent surfaced a direct contradiction. Here, no source contradicted the assumed venue within the evidence cutoff window. The pipeline's venue-checking capability is reactive (catches contradictions in evidence) rather than proactive (verifies venue from authoritative schedule sources). This reveals a structural weakness: when no source happens to mention the correct venue, the error propagates unchecked.

- **The impact sub asymmetry was consequential but unmodeled.** MI used Rohit Sharma as impact sub — he batted at the top (25 off 26, slow but occupying the crease while Rickelton 48(23) attacked). This is a tactical use of the impact sub rule that materially changes MI's batting composition. PBKS used Vishnu Vinod (15* off 8 — useful but minor). The memo mentioned impact subs in "What Would Change" scenarios but didn't model the possibility that MI would use the rule to add Rohit without having him in the starting XI, effectively fielding a 12th player with no downside.

- **Tilak Varma's match-winning ceiling (75* off 33, SR 227) was present in the data but not weighted.** The stats snapshot listed Tilak Varma: 204 runs, SR 148.9 — middling numbers that obscure his explosive ceiling. His 75* was a match-winning acceleration (approximately 50 runs in the last 3 overs with Will Jacks). The memo's batting comparison focused on aggregate stats (Rickelton 380 SR 185.4, Naman Dhir 256 SR 149.7, Rohit 221 SR 170.0) and concluded MI's batting was "feast-or-famine." The feast scenario materialized through Tilak rather than the names the memo highlighted.

- **PBKS's 4th consecutive loss reveals a deeper issue than "losses to strong opponents away from home."** The memo discounted the 3-match losing streak because "all 3 losses were against strong opponents." But extending to 4 straight (now including a home loss to dead-rubber MI) suggests the decline is structural, not contextual. When a team's losing streak extends beyond what opponent-quality adjustment can explain, the streak itself becomes informative — something has changed (confidence, team cohesion, fatigue, tactical predictability). The -1-2pp adjustment for "PBKS losing streak" should have been -3-5pp, reflecting the possibility of genuine decline rather than bad scheduling.

### Skeptic Assessment

The Skeptic's analytical framework was methodologically the strongest in the experiment series — correctly invoking Entries 1, 3, 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12 — but was fatally undermined by operating on wrong venue data.

**Strengths:** (1) Challenge #3 (dead-rubber narrative adds nothing) was fully vindicated — MI won as a dead-rubber team with a weakened lineup, exactly as Entry 7 predicts is possible. This is the Skeptic's most important contribution. (2) Challenge #1 (H2H parity moderates season-form signal) correctly anchored toward 50%. The 17-17 H2H proved more predictive than the 6-3 vs 3-7 season records. (3) Challenge #7 (MI's ceiling is not representative) was partially validated in an unexpected way: MI's actual ceiling performance came from Tilak Varma and Thakur, not from the Rohit + Rickelton ceiling the Skeptic discussed. (4) The anchoring check (starting from 50%, building to 55-57%) was directionally correct — the true probability was closer to 50% than to the market's 61.5%. (5) All learning log entries were correctly assessed for applicability.

**Weaknesses:** (1) All venue-specific analysis (Challenges #2, #5, #6) was for Mullanpur, not Dharamsala. Challenge #2 said "PBKS's all-time record at this venue is 6-8 (42.9% win rate)" — coincidentally the same as their Dharamsala record, so the conclusion (moderate home advantage, not large) happened to be directionally correct for the wrong reason. But Challenge #6's toss analysis (66.7% at the venue) was Mullanpur-specific and may not apply to Dharamsala. (2) The Skeptic's band width recommendation ("standard-to-wide, +/-6-7pp") should have been wider given the venue uncertainty. If the Skeptic had known about or suspected the venue discrepancy, a +/-8-9pp band centered at 54% would have captured the result. (3) Challenge #4 (PBKS's losing streak is contextual) was too generous to PBKS. The Skeptic said "losing to top-4 teams away from home is not the same as general form decline" — but 4 consecutive losses (including this one at home vs a dead-rubber team) suggests genuine decline was already underway.

### Decisive Factor

The match was decided in the death overs by Tilak Varma's unbeaten 75 off 33 balls (6 fours, 6 sixes, SR 227). MI needed approximately 50 from the last 3 overs and Tilak, partnered with Will Jacks (25* off 10), took the match away from PBKS's bowlers. Jansen (4-0-55-1) and Bartlett (3.5-0-53-0) were demolished in the death, leaking 108 runs in their combined 7.5 overs. PBKS posted a competitive 200/8 — driven by Prabhsimran Singh's 57(32) at the top and Omarzai's explosive 38(17) in the lower order — but their death bowling (Jansen economy 13.75, Bartlett economy 13.83) could not defend it.

Shardul Thakur's 4/39 was the key bowling performance. He dismissed Prabhsimran Singh (57), Iyer (4), Shedge (8), and Jansen (2) — with the Prabhsimran-Iyer double strike in the 12th over collapsing PBKS from 107/2 to 111/4, and further wickets dragging them to 156/7. PBKS's last 8 overs yielded only 93/6 — a sharp deceleration from Thakur's intervention. Despite Bumrah's unusually expensive 0/35, MI's bowling collective (Thakur + Chahar 6/75 in 8 overs) contained PBKS to 200 — below the Mullanpur 2026 average of 214 that the memo cited but consistent with Dharamsala's norms.

MI won the toss and bowled — the toss-winner won the match, consistent with the memo's (and Skeptic's) recognition that the toss is consequential at PBKS's home venues. However, this activated the memo's "narrowing" scenario (MI toss win → 50-55% PBKS), which was the most accurate conditional estimate and nearly captured the result.

The memo's core analytical framework was sound: H2H base, moderate adjustments, contrarian lean from market. The directional lean was correct. The catastrophic failure was venue identification — a pipeline-level error that contaminated all venue-specific inputs. Had the memo correctly identified Dharamsala, applied PBKS's 6-8 venue record (removing the +3-4pp home advantage), and perhaps recognized the 4-loss momentum, the band center would have been approximately 53-54% PBKS (47% MI), and MI winning at 47% would have been well within a standard-width band.

## Lessons for Reflection Log

- **The pipeline's venue identification is reactive, not proactive — it catches contradictions in evidence but does not independently verify the venue from authoritative schedule sources.** When no source within the evidence cutoff window mentions the correct venue, errors propagate unchecked. The Exp 6 success (catching Chinnaswamy → Raipur) was due to a lucky contradiction surfaced by the news agent, not a systematic verification step. A proactive venue check (querying the IPL fixture list or official website for today's venue) would have prevented this error entirely. The pipeline needs a venue verification step that is independent of whether the news search happens to surface a contradiction.

- **When a losing streak extends beyond 3 matches and includes a home loss to a weaker team, treat it as a signal of genuine decline rather than contextual noise.** PBKS's 3-match streak was explained away as "losses to strong opponents away from home." The 4th loss (at home vs dead-rubber MI) invalidated that explanation — the home/away and opponent-quality excuses no longer apply. A streak-length threshold of 3+ should trigger a larger adjustment (-3-5pp rather than -1-2pp), especially when the streak crosses the home/away boundary.
