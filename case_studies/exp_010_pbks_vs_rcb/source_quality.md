# Source Quality Assessment: Punjab Kings vs Royal Challengers Bengaluru

## Overall Evidence Quality: Mixed

The News & Conditions Analyst's report draws from a reasonable range of sources — ESPNcricinfo scorecards, IANS wire service, Outlook India, Yahoo Sports, CricketTimes, Crictracker, SportsTak, NewsX, LatestLY, and Business Standard. However, a significant portion of the report rests on predicted XIs from journalists (speculative by definition), undated pitch/venue analyses recycling generic historical data, and weather forecasts from preview articles rather than raw meteorological APIs. The analyst did a commendable job labeling many claims as "SPECULATION" themselves. One key confirmed claim (Patidar ruled out / Jitesh captains) anchors the report. The post-toss XI from Business Standard is potentially the strongest evidence but may fall outside the evidence cutoff, creating a timing problem.

## Flagged Issues

1. **Pervasive undated pitch/venue sources**: Claims 19, 20, 22, 24, 25 rely on undated articles from Yahoo Sports and Crictoday. These read as generic venue guides rather than current-match reporting. No curator statement found. Downstream agents should treat pitch characterization with significant uncertainty.

2. **Phil Salt injury sources all undated (Claim 13)**: Three sources cited but no publication dates for any. Cannot verify whether "not yet cleared" reflects current status or earlier point in timeline.

3. **CricketTimes 403 error (Claims 27, 31)**: Humidity (48%) and wind speed (4 km/h) sourced from inaccessible article via search snippet. Low confidence in these specific numbers.

4. **Post-toss XI timing ambiguity (Claim 17)**: Business Standard live scorecard may contain confirmed RCB XI, but timestamp relative to evidence cutoff is unknown. Toss ~09:30 UTC vs cutoff 09:40 UTC — could be just inside or outside.

5. **Predicted XIs with false precision (Claims 4, 5, 16)**: Full 11-player lists create impression of certainty not warranted. None cite official sources.

6. **Connolly back issue from low-tier source (Claim 6)**: Sunday Guardian Live, single source, no medical bulletin, passive attribution.

7. **LatestLY as source (Claim 42)**: Social-media-adjacent aggregator, undated. Lowest reliability in the report.

8. **Rotation inference unsourced (Claim 36)**: Analyst speculates Patidar absence "may suggest rotation." IANS says "ruled out" — different connotation.

9. **PBKS streak matches 1-3 unsourced (Claim 38)**: Opponents listed without dates or sources.

10. **Generic venue lore vs 2026 data conflict (Claims 19-20 vs 21)**: Historical ~160 avg vs 2026 ~213 avg. The 2026 confirmed data should be weighted far more heavily.
