## Flagged Issues

1. **No official playing XI for either team.** All XI predictions are journalist speculation from Dream11/fantasy preview articles. Downstream agents must treat lineups as uncertain until toss-time confirmation. The disagreement across sources on DC's and RR's overseas slots is real and unresolved.

2. **Sangakkara quote on Parag availability is relayed through a secondary source (cricketaddictor.com), not from a press conference transcript or official RR channel.** The analyst labels this "near-confirmed" which is generous. It should be treated as probable, not confirmed. If this quote turns out to be paraphrased or editorialized, the Parag return claim weakens.

3. **One corroborating source for dew probability (nativeplanet.com) returned HTTP 403.** The analyst could not verify its content. This leaves the "very low dew" claim resting on a single substantive source (sports.yahoo.com) plus seasonal common knowledge.

4. **The Sameer Rizvi "DC management explanation" claim is from thedakia.com (not a top-tier cricket outlet) and is undated/from earlier in the season.** It may be stale — Rizvi's role could have changed since.

5. **cricketaddictor.com Dream11 preview contains internally inconsistent information** (mentions "evening dew" contradicting its own weather data, and lists a DC XI inconsistent with other sources). This outlet's preview appears to be partially template-generated. All claims sourced solely from this outlet should be cross-verified.

6. **Points table data has a noted discrepancy** between the orchestrator-provided context (DC 8 pts / 10 matches) and the analyst's search results (DC 10-12 pts / 12 matches). The analyst flags this honestly, but downstream agents should verify against IPLT20.com before using standings for motivation analysis.

7. **No curator or groundstaff quotes exist for this match.** All pitch characterisation is from journalist previews drawing on historical data. The DC 75 all-out result at this venue this season shows the pitch can behave outside its "batting-friendly" reputation, but no current-condition evidence explains when or why.

8. **Generic venue lore risk:** Multiple claims about the pitch being "batting-friendly" and "black soil" are repeated across sources in near-identical language, suggesting shared/recycled copy rather than independent observation.

**Weight heavily:**

- Match results and head-to-head from May 1 (confirmed, ESPNcricinfo scorecards)
- Weather data: heat, low humidity, negligible rain/dew (probable, IMD-sourced)

- Sunset time, DC home record, points table positions (confirmed/probable)
- KL Rahul 152* and DC 75 all-out at this venue (confirmed match facts demonstrating pitch variance)

**Treat with caution:**
- Parag return/fitness (probable but not officially confirmed pre-toss)
- Sangakkara coaching quotes (secondary source relay)
- Natarajan fitness (genuinely unknown)
- Points table exact numbers (discrepancy flagged)

**Discount or widen uncertainty:**
- All predicted playing XIs (speculative, disagreement across sources, no official confirmation)
- RR overseas slot composition (pure speculation)
- Pitch behaviour predictions ("pace-friendly in evening," "dual nature") — journalist opinion without curator backing
- "Evening dew" from cricketaddictor.com (contradicted by primary weather data)
- Spin bowling effectiveness claims (journalist opinion, Claim 33)

**Structural note for Synthesizer:** Approximately 40% of the analyst's claims are confirmed or anchored in verifiable facts (match results, records, weather data). Approximately 35% are probable (secondary-source relayed quotes, weather forecasts, mathematical standings analysis). The remaining 25% are speculative (playing XI predictions, pitch behaviour forecasts, journalist opinion). The evidence base is adequate for match context but weak on the two most market-relevant questions: actual team composition and pitch conditions on match day. Uncertainty bands should be widened accordingly.
